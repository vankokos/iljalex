STUDY IN POLAND – A MODERN EDUCATION ACCORDING TO EUROPEAN STANDARDS

Polish universities are some of the oldest and most respected educational institutions in Europe, often highly positioned in the top ranks of higher education institutions around the world and are also famous for their academic achievements. Polish universities are both public and non-public institutions, which have similar educational plans and entire teaching process with full academic independence. Both public and private universities in Poland are taught by professors and Doctor of Sciences, professors with long experience and great expertise

THE RESULT OF STUDYING IN POLAND IS MODERN EDUCATION AND A EUROPEAN DIPLOMA RECOGNIZED ALL OVER THE WORLD

Diplomas of Polish universities are recognized in all European Union countries as well as in the CIS countries, including Ukraine and Belarus. This diploma, in turn, allows you to be free to choose the country or city to work and reside in, and gives you the opportunity to develop and apply your knowledge and skills anywhere in the world. Studying at a Polish university means not only a prestigious modern profession and a valuable diploma, but also a bright and rich life

COST OF EDUCATION IN POLAND

Students accepted to study at Polish universities may receive education both on a fee-paying and free of charge basis. The price of education depends on the university itself, the faculty and specialty, the language, and many other factors. There are also certain conditions, under which a foreign student can qualify for free education at a Polish university

LANGUAGE OF INSTRUCTION AND PREPARATORY COURSES

What distinguishes Polish universities from other European universities is the fact that, in the absence of sufficiently high knowledge of the Polish language, many Polish universities offer their applicants the right to study in English. The preparatory course or "zerówka" is designed specifically for the preparation and adaptation of foreign students. The one-year program of this preparatory course helps to master the Polish language at a high level, adapt school curriculum to Polish requirements, get acquainted with Polish culture and traditions, as well as to make friends. The cost of "zerówka" ranges from 1000 to 3000 euros, depending on the number of hours, specialization of core subjects, their number and complexity

POST SECONDARY SCHOOL, BACCALAUREATE AND MAGISTRACY

Schools for adults in Poland are called “szkoła policealna”. Poland offers education not only for young people, but also for older age groups, and make them get a possibility to obtain a European diploma or certificate of completion. Most such courses are free of charge for foreigners. Obtaining higher education at a university in Poland is called a bachelor's degree. Here you are supposed to study after school in the university for 3-4-5 years, depending on the specialty. If you already have a degree, but you want to go further in the study of the profession, including with a scientific approach - higher education training is also available for foreigners
